Common Questions

Do I need representation for a traffic hearing?

While not legally required, having experienced counsel significantly improves outcomes. We navigate procedural complexities, challenge evidence, and negotiate reductions that self-represented individuals rarely achieve.

What should I bring to an initial consultation?

Bring all relevant documents: citations, accident reports, correspondence, insurance policies, and medical records. The more complete your materials, the more precise our assessment and strategy.

How do you evaluate litigation risk?

We analyze case strength, evidence quality, opposing counsel, jurisdictional precedent, and cost-benefit ratios. You receive a candid assessment with clear probability ranges and recommended action.

Will my matter go to trial?

Most cases settle through negotiation. However, we prepare every file for trial from day one. This readiness strengthens our negotiating position and ensures we're never caught unprepared.

How do fees typically work?

Fee structures vary by case type. Personal injury matters often use contingency arrangements. Traffic and litigation cases may use flat fees or hourly billing. We provide transparent quotes during consultation.

How quickly can you respond?

Urgent matters receive same-day attention. Routine inquiries are addressed within 24 hours. Court deadlines and time-sensitive issues always take priority in our workflow.
